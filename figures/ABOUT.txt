What each diagram is (for your presentation)

01_pipeline_overview.png
  One picture of the whole story: image -> clean outline -> follow the border
  and count directions -> turn into numbers -> pick the closest "average" shape.

02_prototype_fingerprints.png
  What the program learned: left = how often each of 8 directions appears on
  the border (per class). Right = "roundness" of the outline (extra number).

03_confusion_matrix.png
  Rows = true shape, columns = what the program guessed. Big numbers on the
  diagonal = good; off-diagonal = mistakes.

04_roundness_by_class.png
  Training shapes: disks tend to be "rounder" than squares and triangles.
  This is why that extra number helps tell a messy circle from a triangle.

05_sample_results.png
  A few training images and a few test images with true label vs guess.

06_enhancement_filters.png
  From the ABL / enhancement part: noisy image vs histogram equalize vs
  median vs Gaussian blur (not the main classifier, but easy to show).
